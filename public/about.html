FAQ

1 - How does it work?

Say you open www.google.com, type the letter A on the search box and write down whatever Google suggests you to complete the A with. Now you type B, write down the suggestions and so on, until Z.

After that, you go to Google Images and do everything again, from A to Z. And then repeat, this time using Youtube.

Once this is done, you change your language settings on Google from English — or whatever your native language is — to a different idiom. And then you repeat the whole thing, google.com A-Z, Google Images A-Z, Youtube A-Z...

That's how it works, but a computer does all that, instead of a person. And it is done everyday, for 22 different languages.

2 - How did you choose those languages?

First, I've chosen the ones that are based on the latin alphabet, so I could use the A-Z method. Second, I've checked their Autocomplete predictions. Not all of them work right for the location I am in. And not all services work for all languages.

Google Web and Images predictions are from 22 languages: Danish, German, English, Spanish, Finnish, French, Croatian, Hungarian, Indonesian, Icelandic, Italian, Lithuanian, Dutch, Norwegian, Polish, Portuguese (Brasil), Portuguese, Romanian, Slovak, Swedish, Turkish, and Vietnamese.

Youtube videos shown here are from 6 languages only: German, English, Spanish, French, Italian, and Brazilian Portuguese.

3 - Why are my Google suggestions different from the ones I see in your archive?

Mostly because your suggestions are based on your search and browsing history. They are highlighted in blue and show up first in the list.

4 - Doesn't the same happen to the computer that is doing your archive?

No, because Google save your information in your account or on your browser. The computer doing the archive doesn't have either of those — not even a browser. The way I described things on #1 was just to help you understand the process, but it's not exactly how it works.

5 - What about my location?

Yes, that affects both yours and mine results. That's the reason why I can't get results for any language (see #2).
